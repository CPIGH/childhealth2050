## At a glance

Kenya is an East African country that has made notable gains in child survival over the past two
decades, though outcomes still vary widely across its regions.

## Recent progress

Under-five and infant mortality have declined substantially since the early 2000s, helped by rising
immunization coverage and improved access to maternal and child services. Neonatal mortality has
improved more slowly and remains a priority.

## Equity

Gaps by wealth and geography persist: arid and semi-arid counties and the poorest households
continue to see higher child mortality than the national average.

## Financing & context

Health financing has shifted gradually toward domestic and insurance-based mechanisms, but
out-of-pocket costs and county-level differences in capacity still affect access.
