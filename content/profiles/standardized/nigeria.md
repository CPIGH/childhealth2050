## At a glance

Nigeria is the most populous country in sub-Saharan Africa and accounts for a large share of the
region's under-five deaths, so its trajectory weighs heavily on global child-health goals.

## Recent progress

Under-five mortality has declined over recent decades, but progress has been uneven and slower than
in several regional peers. Neonatal mortality has fallen more slowly than the overall under-five
rate, leaving newborn deaths a growing share of the total.

## Equity

Large gaps persist by household wealth, mother's education, and urban versus rural residence, with
the poorest and most rural populations seeing markedly worse outcomes.

## Financing & context

Out-of-pocket spending makes up a high share of health financing, and regional insecurity and
service-access gaps in parts of the country shape both coverage and outcomes.

## Outlook to 2050

On current trends Nigeria is unlikely to reach the strongest child-survival targets without faster
progress, particularly on newborn survival. The detailed forecast lives in Future Trajectories.

## Key caveats

Estimates draw on periodic household surveys and modelled series; the most recent survey vintage and
sub-national variation should be kept in mind when reading any single figure.
